Introduction

The CONSENSE project (Contributing to sustainable energy systems in Norway: quantifying life-cycle impacts on biodiversity) aims to assess the overall impacts of the Norwegian power generation and distribution on biodiversity by using a holistic life cycle perspective, both for today’s electricity system and for future production scenarios.

The main objectives of the project are:

  • Develop a life cycle impact assessment model for quantifying impacts of pathways of power lines on Norwegian biodiversity (focusing on mammals and birds), and apply them to the Norwegian electricity transmission system of today
  • Assess the total biodiversity impact of energy production and transmission within the current Norwegian electricity supply-demand network
  • Evaluate potential biodiversity impacts of future renewable electricity production and distribution scenarios in Norway

DatabasesRelevant Information
Artsdatabanken (NO)En nasjonal kunnskapsbank for naturmangfold. Hovedoppgave er å formidle oppdatert og lett tilgjengeleg informasjon om artar og naturtypar / A national knowledge bank for biodiversity. The main task is to disseminate updated and easily accessible information about species and habitat types.
BIOFRAGA global database for analysing biodiversity responses to forest fragmentation.
BirdLife InternationalBirdLife International contains various data about birds, e.g. species, red lists, IBA (sites), distribution maps and case studies.
Copernicus Land Monitoring Servicegeographical information on land cover to a broad range of users in the field of environmental terrestrial applications. This includes land use, land cover characteristics and changes, vegetation state, water cycle and earth surface energy variables.
GBIFThe Global Biodiversity Information Facility is an international network and data infrastructure and provides open access to data about all types of life on Earth. Access database with location data for more than 1.6 million species.
GeoNorge (NO)Geonorge er det nasjonale nettstedet for kartdata og annen stedfestet informasjon i Norge. Her kan brukere av kartdata søke etter og få tilgang til det som er tilgjengelig av slik informasjon / Geonorge is the national website for map data and other location information in Norway. Here, users of map data can search for and access what is available from such information.
IRENAIRENA’s renewable electricity capacity and generation statistics and query the dataset to select countries, years and technologies (available for the year 2000 onwards and cover all countries in the world).
IUCNThe IUCN Red List provides information about threatened species. Assigned threat levels range from least concern to extinct. The database can be searched for mammal species.
Kartverket (NO)Norwegian geodata: basic datasets of Norway, terrain, boundaries and remote sensing data.
NVE Kartkatalog (NO)Geodata from the Norges vassdrags- og energidirektorat (Norwegian Water Resources and Energy Directorate), including data about hydro-power and wind power in Norway.
PREDICTSA global database of how local terrestrial biodiversity responds to human impacts.
TheWindPowerA comprehensive database of detailed raw statistics on the rapidly growing sphere of wind energy. The database can be searched for wind power plants in Norway (coordinates, commission year, number of turbines, total power, etc.).
