Industrial Ecology Digital Lab : EXIOBASE

Created by Unknown User (radekl), last modified by Konstantin Stadler on 01.09.2021

EXIOBASE3: Is a time series set of MRIO tables based on trade-linked supply-use tables, and currently is estimated from 1995-2022 (where more recent and future years are "nowcasted").

Current developments are being undertaken in the folder:

X:\indecol\Projects\EXIOBASE_dev

 - a lot of the source data for the SUTs, trade data, extensions, etc is in this folder.

By and large, this folder should be considered read-only - discuss any changes with Richard Wood and/or Konstantin Stadler.

The "compiled" data can be found at X:\indecol\Projects\MRIOs - this has been stored by version number. Originally, this was only the MRIO tables, but we have started including additional underlying data in the folder as well.


Public vs Private versions

The private version has aggregated energy and material accounts because of licensing issues with the IEA. Land accounts are also aggregated due to wishes of BOKU (partner).

If you do not need these details for your analysis, it is recommend to use the public version. This is also available on Zenodo with a doi for each version.

The public version is licensed under CC-BY 4.0 - so you can share it freely and other can reuse it given proper attribution. The private version can only be shared with partners holding an IEA license - discuss these cases with Edgar Hertwich , Richard Wood or Konstantin Stadler .


History

EXIOBASE 3 is the culmination of work in the FP7 DESIRE project (www.fp7desire.eu) and builds upon earlier work on EXIOBASE 2 in the FP7 CREEA projected (www.creea.eu) and the FP6 EXIOPOL (EXIOBASE 1) project. See Stadler at al. 2018 - Table 1 for a comprehensive overview about the different EXIOBASE versions. This paper is part of a special issue on EXIOBASE with many more articles on the development and use of EXIOBASE.

Older versions of EXIOBASE are available on  www.exiobase.eu, newer versions are on  Zenodo. We also keep public and private versions on a EXIOBASE box account for easy sharing with collaborators.

Documentation

The main data documentation is described See Stadler at al. 2018. This corresponds to version 3.4 of the data for the time series 1995-2011.

End-year/Nowcasting

We  also have estimates based on trade and macro-economic data up to 2016  as well, however, a lot of care must be taken in use of this data. It is not suitable for analyzing trends over time. The basic description of the process employed is in the relevant deliverable:

http://fp7desire.eu/documents/category/3-public-deliverables?download=24:d5-3-integrated-report-on-ee-io-related-macro-resource-indicator-time-series


Data quality/adherence

In terms of data quality for more recent years. Please be mindful of:

  • 2007 is the base year for the SUTs  and the method of interpolation over time involves using data in  different classifications and formats to understand structural change  over time (i.e. intermediate and final demand flows by product and  industry). Only output, trade and macroeconomic constraints (7 sectors)  are direct inputs to final balancing in absolute terms. The database is  under the NACE1 classification scheme.
  • national SUT and IOT data are only used to inform structural change
  • Whilst the 2007 EXIOBASE2 table is  the base, the National Account Main Aggregates is the main constraint.  The main goal was temporal consistency, and not disaggregation of  official tables in EXIO3. SUTs/IOTs do often not get updated going  backwards, whilst National Account Main Aggregates data does. In  general, National Account Main Aggregates are often 10-20% off the  equivalent data points aggregated from the official SUTs/IOTs from  2007.

Energy & emission accounts

Energy  and Emission accounts are based on the IEA energy balances, but are not always fully consistent with the monetary IO tables (different data  sources was used in the disaggregation of the energy accounts) – this  does lead to some minor discrepancies in relationships between energy  flows and monetary flows. Some efforts are ongoing to fix these.

Household  use of energy – this is available in disaggregated form (by EXIOBASE  product), but is auxiliary to the main MRIO dataset, as it does not fit  that data structure. Similar case for fuel combustion emissions.

Work is being undertaken to update the energy and emission accounts to make them more consistent with the monetary data (ask Kajwan or Richard).
